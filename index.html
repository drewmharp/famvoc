What We Do

The Family Voice:

We support Families who want to “Find Their Voice” and strengthen advocacy skills, using a collaborative and non-adversarial approach. We explain legal rights but are unable to offer any legal advice. Our Families:

  • Learn information and connect with resources
  • Build Relationships
  • Understand goals, options and legal rights
  • Take Action
  • Reflect and make adjustments along the way
  • Strengthen Resiliency
  • Exercise self-care

Families We Support:

Using a collaborative and non-adversarial approach, we support Families facing all kinds of challenges:

  • Learning or behavior issues in school (special education, 504 plans, etc
  • School discipline (truancy, suspension or expulsion)
  • Mental Health and Substance Abuse
  • Juvenile Justice
  • Education & Employment for transition age youth ages 13-25

We support Families to use self-care while strengthening Relationships, Reflection and Resilience

Important Information to Know: This website uses the term "parent" to include many different people in a child’s life; including birth or adoptive parents; grandparents, aunts, uncles or any other adult individual with whom the child lives and is acting as the parent; foster parents; or legal guardians.
